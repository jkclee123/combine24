🔢 How combine24 Handles Equivalent Solutions

Discover how our smart algorithm recognizes mathematically equivalent expressions.

🎯 The Core Concept

Different-looking formulas can be mathematically equivalent. Our app converts every expression into a standardized "schema" before comparison, treating equivalent solutions as the same.

Example with numbers: 3, 8, 6, 7

3 + 8 + 6 + 7
= 24
3 + 6 + 7 + 8
= 24
✓ Considered Equivalent

➕ Commutative Operations (+ and ×)

Addition and multiplication are commutative - the order of numbers doesn't change the result. Our schema represents these as unordered groups.

Cards: 2, 3, 4, 6

(2 × 4) + (3 × 6)
= 8 + 18 = 24
(6 × 3) + (4 × 2)
= 18 + 8 = 24
✓ Same Solution

Both expressions are recognized as "sum of two products"

➖ Non-Commutative Operations (- and ÷)

Subtraction and division are not commutative - order matters! Our schema preserves the order for these operations.

Cards: 2, 4, 6, 8

6 × 8 ÷ (4 - 2)
= 48 ÷ 2 = 24
✓ Valid Solution

Different Order = Different Result

(4 - 2) ÷ (6 × 8)
= 2 ÷ 48 = 0.0417
✗ Different Result

Schema treats these as distinct expressions

📐 Parentheses & Precedence

The app respects mathematical order of operations: parentheses first, then multiplication/division, then addition/subtraction.

Cards: 1, 2, 3, 4

(1 + 2 + 3) × 4
= 6 × 4 = 24
✓ Forces addition first

Different Structure

1 + 2 + (3 × 4)
= 1 + 2 + 12 = 15
✗ Different calculation

Equivalent Rearrangement

4 × (3 + 2 + 1)
= 4 × 6 = 24
✓ Same as first example

Order in addition and main groups doesn't matter

1️⃣
Structure Match: Both schemas have identical nested operations (- → × → -)

J × 8 ÷ 4 + 2

🔍 How It Works

  1. Parse: Convert infix notation to structured tree representation
  2. Normalize: Apply commutative rules for + and × operations
  3. Compare: Use unOrdDeepEq to recursively compare schema structures
  4. Deduplicate: Treat equivalent schemas as the same solution
